# Interview Questions
Stakeholder: Researcher

Justification:Researchers are the primary users of the system and are directly involved in submitting, revising, and tracking ethics applications. They face issues such as delays, lack of transparency, and inconsistent document management. Interviewing them helps uncover gaps in the current workflow and refine system requirements for submission processes, status tracking, and communication mechanisms.

## Question 1: How do you currently prepare and submit ethics applications?
- Do you typically submit all required documents at once, or in multiple stages?
- Would a step-by-step submission process be more helpful to you, or would you prefer to complete everything on one page?
- Do you usually know before submission whether all required documents are included, or would a checklist in the system be useful?

Uncertainty: The current workflow is unclear and may vary between researchers.
Purpose: To understand existing processes and identify inefficiencies.
Impact: May lead to requirements for standardised submission workflows.


## Question 2: What challenges do you face when tracking the status of your application?
- Are you currently able to see the status of your application without contacting administrators?
- What level of detail would you expect to see, such as current stage, reviewer comments, or next required action?
- Would it be useful if the system automatically notified you whenever the application status changes?

Uncertainty: The exact issues with transparency are unknown.
Purpose: To identify whether tracking features are needed.
Impact: May require a status dashboard or notification system.

## Question 3: How do you manage updates to your supporting documents?
- Should previous versions of documents remain accessible after updates?
- When you upload a revised document, would it be useful for the system to record who made the change and when it was made?
- Would it help if the system required a short explanation whenever a new version of a document is uploaded?

Uncertainty: Document version control practices are unclear.
Purpose: To understand how document updates are handled.
Impact: May require version control functionality.

## Question 4: Have you experienced delays in the approval process? What caused them?
- At which stage of the process do delays most commonly occur: submission, review, or communication?
- When delays happen, are you usually told the reason, or do you need to contact someone to find out?
- Would automatic reminders or deadlines for pending actions help reduce delays in your experience?

Uncertainty: The reasons for delays are not fully understood.
Purpose: To identify bottlenecks in the process.
Impact: May require workflow optimisation.

## Question 5: What type of communication do you receive during the review process, and how effective is it?
- Should communication between researchers and reviewers be handled within the system or via external tools such as email?
- Would it be helpful if all comments, clarification requests, and decisions were stored in one place within the application record?
- Should the system distinguish between formal decisions, revision requests, and general messages?

Uncertainty: It is unclear whether communication issues are caused by the lack of a centralised system or by inconsistent communication practices.
Purpose: To understand how communication currently occurs and whether it meets researchers’ needs.
Impact: May lead to requirements for standardised communication channels or integrated messaging and notification features.

## Question 6: What difficulties do you face when preparing and organising the documents required for an ethics application?
- Do you follow a standard structure when preparing documents, or does it vary each time?
- Are you always clear about which documents are mandatory, or would templates and document categories be helpful?
- Would it help if the system showed which required documents are missing, complete, or still need revision?

Uncertainty: It is unclear how researchers manage required documents and whether the process is inefficient or confusing.
Purpose:To understand challenges related to document preparation and organisation.
Impact: May lead to requirements for document templates, structured submission forms, or improved document management features.
