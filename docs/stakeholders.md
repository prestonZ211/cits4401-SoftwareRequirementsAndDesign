# Stakeholder Identification

## 1. Researchers (Including Students and Academic Staff)
**the stakeholder’s Role:**  
Researchers are responsible for preparing and submitting ethics applications, including providing required documents and responding to feedback during the review process.
**Value or Interests from the System:**  
Researchers want a simple and efficient way to submit applications without unnecessary delays. 
They need clear visibility of the application status and timely feedback, so they can plan and progress their research with confidence.

## 2. Ethics Committee Members
**the stakeholder’s Role:**  
Ethics committee members review applications, assess risks, request revisions if needed, and make decisions such as approval or rejection.
**Value or Interests from the System:** 
They need a more efficient and organised way to review applications. 
This includes having access to complete and up-to-date documents, being able to discuss and review applications easily, and managing conflicts of interest properly.

## 3. Administrative Staff
**the stakeholder’s Role:**  
Administrative staff handle initial checks, track application progress, and coordinate communication between researchers and committee members.
**Value or Interests from the System:** 
They want a more efficient and reliable way to manage and track applications. 
This includes having a centralised system that reduces manual work, minimises errors, and provides clear visibility of application status.
